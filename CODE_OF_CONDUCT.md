# Code of Conduct

mesoSPIM is a community of people who build, break, fix and share microscopes. It
works because people are generous with what they know. This document is a short note
on how we'd like to keep it that way.

## The short version

Be kind. Assume good faith. Share what you learn.

## In practice

Our community spans people who design optics for a living and people switching on a
light-sheet microscope for the first time. Both belong here.

- **Beginner questions are welcome.** Everyone was new once, usually more recently
  than they admit. Answering patiently is as much a contribution as writing code.
- **Share the things that didn't work**, not just the things that did. A config that
  broke your setup, an alignment that never converged, a dead end — these save other
  people weeks.
- **Criticize code and ideas, not people.** Say "this will lock the GUI" rather than
  "you don't understand threading."
- **Credit people.** If someone's idea, config or debugging got you somewhere, say so.
- **Remember there's hardware at the other end.** Advice that sounds clever can cost
  someone a real objective or a real sample. If you're not sure, say you're not sure.

Not welcome, and hopefully obvious: harassment of any kind, personal attacks,
sexualized language or imagery, sharing someone's private information, or being
persistently unpleasant in a way that makes people stop asking questions.

## If something goes wrong

We're a small project without a moderation team, so this is informal.

Most friction is a misunderstanding — often between people writing in a second or
third language, across very different backgrounds. If you can sort it out directly and
in good faith, that's usually best.

If you can't, or if it's something you shouldn't have to handle yourself, contact the
maintainers at **info@mesospim.org**. We'll listen, keep it private, and do
something about it. Depending on what happened, that might be a quiet word, asking
someone to step back from a thread, or removing them from the project's spaces. We'd
much rather never need the last one.

## Where this applies

The [GitHub repositories](https://github.com/mesoSPIM), the

That's it. Now go build something.
